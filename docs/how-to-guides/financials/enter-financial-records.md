# How to Enter Financial Records

**Input your P&L and balance sheet data for valuation and tracking.**

*Time required: 30-60 minutes for initial entry, 15 minutes monthly thereafter*

---

## Why Enter Financial Records?

Accurate financial data enables:
- **Exit Readiness Score** calculation
- **Valuation estimates** based on your numbers
- **Trend analysis** to show growth
- **Buyer confidence** with organized financials
- **Benchmark comparisons** to industry standards

---

## Before You Start

### Gather Your Financial Statements

**Required** (minimum):
- Profit & Loss (P&L) statements - Last 12 months
- Balance sheets - Last 12 months

**Recommended**:
- P&L statements - Last 3 years
- Balance sheets - Last 3 years
- Cash flow statements - Last 3 years

**Ideal**:
- Monthly financials - Last 3-5 years
- Year-end audited statements (if available)

### Prepare Your Data

**From your accounting system**:
- QuickBooks: Reports → Company & Financial
- Xero: Accounting → Reports
- Excel: Organize by month/year

**What you'll need**:
- Revenue by month
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
- Operating expenses by category
- Assets and liabilities
- Cash position

---

## Step 1: Navigate to Financials

1. Log in to Legacy Architect OS
2. Click **"Financials"** in left sidebar
3. Click **"Financial Records"** tab

---

## Step 2: Select Time Period

### Choose Entry Method

**Option A: Enter by Month** (Recommended)
- More detailed
- Shows seasonality
- Better for trend analysis
- Takes longer initially

**Option B: Enter by Year**
- Faster for historical data
- Less detailed
- Good for older years
- Can add monthly detail later

### Select Year

1. Click **"Add Financial Data"**
2. Select year (e.g., 2024, 2023, 2022)
3. Choose monthly or annual entry
4. Click **"Next"**

---

## Step 3: Enter Profit & Loss Data

### Revenue Section

**Total Revenue**:
- Gross revenue before any deductions
- Include all revenue streams
- Example: $500,000

**Revenue by Category** (optional but recommended):
- Product sales
- Service revenue
- Recurring revenue
- One-time revenue

**Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)**:
- Direct costs to deliver product/service
- Materials, labor, shipping
- Example: $200,000

**Gross Profit** (auto-calculated):
- Revenue - COGS
- Example: $300,000

**Gross Margin %** (auto-calculated):
- (Gross Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100
- Example: 60%

### Operating Expenses

**Personnel Costs**:
- Salaries and wages
- Payroll taxes
- Benefits
- Example: $150,000

**Sales & Marketing**:
- Advertising
- Marketing campaigns
- Sales commissions
- Example: $30,000

**General & Administrative**:
- Rent
- Utilities
- Insurance
- Office supplies
- Professional fees (legal, accounting)
- Example: $50,000

**Technology**:
- Software subscriptions
- IT services
- Website hosting
- Example: $10,000

**Other Operating Expenses**:
- Travel
- Training
- Miscellaneous
- Example: $10,000

**Total Operating Expenses** (auto-calculated):
- Sum of all expense categories
- Example: $250,000

### EBITDA Calculation

**Operating Income** (auto-calculated):
- Gross Profit - Operating Expenses
- Example: $50,000

**Add Back**:
- Depreciation
- Amortization
- Example: $10,000

**EBITDA** (auto-calculated):
- Operating Income + Depreciation + Amortization
- Example: $60,000

**EBITDA Margin %** (auto-calculated):
- (EBITDA ÷ Revenue) × 100
- Example: 12%

### Below the Line

**Interest Expense**:
- Loan interest
- Line of credit interest
- Example: $5,000

**Taxes**:
- Income taxes paid
- Example: $10,000

**Net Income** (auto-calculated):
- EBITDA - Interest - Taxes - Depreciation - Amortization
- Example: $35,000

---

## Step 4: Enter Balance Sheet Data

### Assets

**Current Assets**:
- **Cash**: Bank account balances
- **Accounts Receivable**: Money owed by customers
- **Inventory**: Products for sale
- **Prepaid Expenses**: Prepaid rent, insurance, etc.
- **Other Current Assets**

**Total Current Assets** (auto-calculated)

**Fixed Assets**:
- **Property**: Real estate owned
- **Equipment**: Machinery, vehicles, computers
- **Less: Accumulated Depreciation**
- **Net Fixed Assets** (auto-calculated)

**Other Assets**:
- Intangible assets (patents, trademarks)
- Investments
- Deposits

**Total Assets** (auto-calculated)

### Liabilities

**Current Liabilities**:
- **Accounts Payable**: Money owed to suppliers
- **Accrued Expenses**: Wages, taxes owed
- **Current Portion of Long-Term Debt**: Due within 12 months
- **Other Current Liabilities**

**Total Current Liabilities** (auto-calculated)

**Long-Term Liabilities**:
- **Long-Term Debt**: Loans, mortgages
- **Deferred Revenue**: Prepaid by customers
- **Other Long-Term Liabilities**

**Total Liabilities** (auto-calculated)

### Equity

**Owner's Equity**:
- Beginning equity
- Plus: Net income
- Less: Owner distributions
- **Ending Equity** (auto-calculated)

**Total Liabilities + Equity** (auto-calculated)

**Balance Check**: Should equal Total Assets

---

## Step 5: Review and Save

### Verify Your Numbers

**Check**:
- ✅ Balance sheet balances (Assets = Liabilities + Equity)
- ✅ Revenue and expenses are reasonable
- ✅ No obvious typos (extra zeros, decimal errors)
- ✅ Gross margin is realistic for your industry
- ✅ EBITDA margin makes sense

### Save Your Data

1. Click **"Save"** button
2. Data is saved immediately
3. You can edit anytime

---

## Step 6: Repeat for Additional Periods

### Monthly Entry

If entering monthly data:
1. Click **"Add Month"**
2. Select next month
3. Enter data
4. Save
5. Repeat for all 12 months

**Tip**: Copy previous month as starting point
- Click **"Copy from Previous Month"**
- Adjust numbers that changed
- Saves time on recurring expenses

### Historical Years

For previous years:
1. Click **"Add Year"**
2. Select year (2023, 2022, 2021, etc.)
3. Enter annual or monthly data
4. Save

**Recommendation**: Enter at least 3 years of data for trend analysis

## Best Practices

### Accuracy is Critical

**Double-check your numbers**:
- Match to tax returns
- Match to accounting system
- Have accountant review
- Verify calculations

**Why**: Buyers will scrutinize financials. Errors raise red flags.

### Consistency Matters

**Use same accounting method**:
- Cash basis vs. accrual
- Don't switch mid-stream
- Document which you use

**Categorize consistently**:
- Same expenses in same categories
- Don't move things around
- Makes trends meaningful

### Monthly is Better Than Annual

**Advantages of monthly data**:
- Shows seasonality
- Identifies trends faster
- More impressive to buyers
- Better for your own management

**When to use annual**:
- Historical data (3+ years ago)
- Don't have monthly detail
- Can add monthly later

### Update Regularly

**Recommended schedule**:
- **Monthly**: Enter previous month's data
- **Quarterly**: Review trends and accuracy
- **Annually**: Enter full year, review 3-year trends

**Set reminders**: Calendar alerts for data entry

## Common Questions

### What if I don't have all this data?

**Start with what you have**:
- Enter revenue and basic expenses
- Add detail over time
- Work with accountant to fill gaps

**Minimum viable**:
- Total revenue
- Total expenses
- Net income

### Should I use cash or accrual accounting?

**Accrual is preferred** for business sales:
- Matches revenue to when earned
- Matches expenses to when incurred
- More accurate picture of business

**Cash basis is acceptable** if:
- That's what you've always used
- Your tax returns use cash basis
- You're consistent

**Be consistent**: Don't switch methods

### How do I handle one-time expenses?

**Enter them normally**, then:
- Document as EBITDA add-back
- Explain in notes
- Show normalized EBITDA

See: [How to Document EBITDA Add-Backs](document-ebitda-addbacks.md)

### What if my books are a mess?

**Options**:
1. **Clean them up first** (recommended)
   - Hire bookkeeper
   - Get caught up
   - Then enter clean data

2. **Enter what you have**
   - Note data quality issues
   - Plan to improve
   - Show buyers your plan

3. **Get professional help**
   - CPA can reconstruct
   - Quality of Earnings report
   - Costs $5K-$20K but worth it

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: Balance sheet doesn't balance

**Check**:
1. **Assets = Liabilities + Equity?**
2. **Did you enter all categories?**
3. **Any typos or decimal errors?**
4. **Match to your accounting system**

**Solution**: Review each line item carefully

### Problem: Numbers seem wrong

**Verify**:
1. **Match to tax return** (most reliable source)
2. **Match to accounting system reports**
3. **Check for decimal errors** ($10,000 vs. $10,000.00)
4. **Verify you're using same accounting method**

### Problem: Can't find certain expense categories

**Solutions**:
1. **Use "Other" category** and specify in notes
2. **Combine similar expenses** (e.g., all marketing together)
3. **Contact support** for category additions

### Problem: Data entry is taking too long

**Shortcuts**:
1. **Copy previous month** and adjust
2. **Enter annual first**, add monthly detail later
3. **Upload from accounting system** (if integration available)
4. **Delegate to bookkeeper** (invite as team member)
