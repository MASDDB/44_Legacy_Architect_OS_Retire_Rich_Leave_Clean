# How to Track KPI Metrics

**Monitor key performance indicators to demonstrate operational excellence.**

*Time required: 30 minutes initial setup, 15 minutes monthly updates*

---

## What Are KPIs?

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)** are measurable values that show how effectively your business is achieving key objectives.

**Why track them?**
- **Demonstrate performance** to buyers
- **Identify trends** early
- **Make data-driven decisions**
- **Improve Exit Readiness Score**
- **Show operational maturity**

---

## Essential KPIs by Category

### Financial KPIs

**Revenue Metrics**:
- Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
- Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)
- Revenue Growth Rate (YoY, MoM)

- Gross Margin %

- Operating Margin %

**Cash Flow Metrics**:

**Retention Metrics**:

- Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

**Satisfaction Metrics**:
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)

### Operational KPIs

**Efficiency Metrics**:

**Quality Metrics**:
- Defect Rate
- On-Time Delivery %
- First-Time Fix Rate
- Customer Complaint Rate

**Team Metrics**:
- Employee Turnover Rate

- Employee Satisfaction Score

### Sales & Marketing KPIs

**Pipeline Metrics**:

**Marketing Metrics**:
- Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs)

## Step 1: Select Your KPIs

### Choose 5-10 Core KPIs

**Don't track everything**:
- Focus on what matters most
- Align with business goals
- Choose measurable metrics
- Ensure data is available

### Recommended Starter Set

**For Most Businesses**:
1. Revenue Growth Rate (MoM)
2. Gross Margin %
3. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
4. Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
5. Customer Retention Rate
6. Cash Flow (monthly)

**Add based on your business**:
- SaaS: MRR, Churn Rate, NRR
- E-commerce: Average Order Value, Cart Abandonment Rate
- Services: Utilization Rate, Billable Hours %
- Manufacturing: Inventory Turnover, Production Efficiency

## Step 2: Navigate to KPI Tracking

1. Log in to Legacy Architect OS
2. Click **"Financials"** in left sidebar
3. Click **"KPI Tracking"** tab
4. Click **"Set Up KPIs"** button

---

## Step 3: Configure Your KPIs

### Add New KPI

1. Click **"Add KPI"** button
2. Fill in KPI details:

**KPI Name**:
- Clear, descriptive name
- Example: "Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)"

**Category**:
- Financial, Customer, Operational, or Sales & Marketing

**Measurement Type**:
- Currency ($)
- Percentage (%)
- Number (#)
- Ratio (X:1)

**Target Value**:
- Your goal for this KPI
- Example: $100,000 MRR

**Frequency**:
- Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Quarterly, or Annual

**Calculation Method** (optional):
- How you calculate it
- Example: "Sum of all active subscription values"

**Data Source**:
- Where you get the data
- Example: "Stripe dashboard", "QuickBooks report"

3. Click **"Save KPI"**

### Repeat for All KPIs

Add all 5-10 of your core KPIs following the same process.

---

## Step 4: Enter Historical Data

### Why Enter Historical Data?

**Show trends**:
- Buyers want to see trajectory
- Identify patterns
- Demonstrate improvement

**How far back?**
- Minimum: 12 months
- Recommended: 24 months
- Ideal: 36 months

### Enter Data Points

For each KPI:

1. Click on the KPI name
2. Click **"Add Data Point"**
3. Enter:
   - **Date**: When measured
   - **Value**: The actual number
   - **Notes**: Any context (optional)
4. Click **"Save"**

**Tip**: Enter monthly data points for the past 12-24 months

## Step 6: Monitor and Update

### Monthly KPI Review

**First week of each month**:

1. Navigate to KPI Tracking
2. Click **"Add Data Point"** for each KPI
3. Enter previous month's values
4. Add notes for any anomalies
5. Review trends

### What to Look For

**Positive Trends**:
- ✅ Revenue growing
- ✅ Margins improving
- ✅ CAC decreasing
- ✅ Retention increasing

**Warning Signs**:
- ⚠️ Revenue declining
- ⚠️ Margins shrinking
- ⚠️ CAC increasing
- ⚠️ Churn accelerating

**Take Action**:
- Investigate causes
- Implement fixes
- Document improvements
- Show buyers you're proactive

## Calculating Common KPIs

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Formula**:
```
CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Costs ÷ Number of New Customers
```

**Example**:
- Sales & Marketing: $10,000/month
- New Customers: 20/month
- CAC = $10,000 ÷ 20 = $500

**Target**: Should be < 1/3 of Customer Lifetime Value

### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

**Formula**:
```
CLV = Average Revenue Per Customer × Average Customer Lifespan
```

**Example**:
- ARPC: $1,000/year
- Average Lifespan: 5 years
- CLV = $1,000 × 5 = $5,000

**Target**: Should be > 3x Customer Acquisition Cost

### Customer Retention Rate

**Formula**:
```
Retention Rate = ((Customers End - New Customers) ÷ Customers Start) × 100
```

**Example**:
- Customers at start: 100
- Customers at end: 105
- New customers: 10
- Retention = ((105 - 10) ÷ 100) × 100 = 95%

**Target**: > 90% for most businesses

### Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)

**Formula**:
```
MRR = Sum of all monthly subscription values
```

**Example**:
- 50 customers at $100/month = $5,000
- 20 customers at $200/month = $4,000
- MRR = $9,000

**Growth Target**: 10-20% month-over-month for high-growth

### Gross Margin %

**Formula**:
```
Gross Margin % = ((Revenue - COGS) ÷ Revenue) × 100
```

**Example**:
- Revenue: $100,000
- COGS: $40,000
- Gross Margin = (($100,000 - $40,000) ÷ $100,000) × 100 = 60%

**Target**: Varies by industry (50-80% for services, 20-40% for retail)

## Best Practices

### Choose Meaningful KPIs

**Good KPIs**:
- Directly tied to business goals
- Actionable (you can influence them)
- Measurable with available data
- Understandable to stakeholders

**Avoid Vanity Metrics**:
- Total registered users (if most are inactive)
- Page views (without conversion context)
- Social media followers (without engagement)

### Set Realistic Targets

**How to set targets**:
1. **Industry benchmarks**: Research your industry
2. **Historical performance**: Improve on your baseline
3. **Strategic goals**: Align with business objectives
4. **Buyer expectations**: What buyers look for

**Example**:
- Current: 5% monthly revenue growth
- Industry average: 10%
- Target: 8% (realistic improvement)

### Update Consistently

**Consistency is key**:
- Same day each month
- Same data source
- Same calculation method
- Document any changes

**Why**: Inconsistent data is worse than no data

### Add Context with Notes

**When to add notes**:
- Unusual spike or drop
- One-time events
- Seasonal variations
- External factors

**Example**:
- "Revenue up 30% due to holiday promotion"
- "Churn increased 5% - lost major client (non-recurring)"
- "CAC down 20% - new referral program launched"

## Using KPIs for Exit Readiness

### Demonstrate Performance

**Buyers want to see**:
- Consistent growth trends
- Improving margins
- Strong retention
- Efficient operations

**Show them**:
- 24-36 months of KPI data
- Upward trends
- Comparison to industry benchmarks
- Your improvement initiatives

### Improve Your Score

**KPI tracking improves**:
- **Operational Score**: Shows you manage by data
- **Financial Score**: Demonstrates financial discipline
- **Market Score**: Proves competitive performance

**Impact**: Can increase Exit Readiness Score by 5-10 points

### Support Valuation

**KPIs that increase value**:
- High gross margins (60%+)
- Low churn (<5% monthly for SaaS)
- Strong CLV:CAC ratio (>3:1)
- Consistent revenue growth (10%+ annually)

**Example**:
- Business A: No KPI tracking, claims "doing well"
- Business B: 36 months of KPIs showing 15% growth, 70% margins
- **Business B gets 20-30% higher valuation**

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake #1: Tracking Too Many KPIs

**Problem**: Overwhelmed, can't focus

**Solution**: Start with 5-10 core KPIs, expand later

### Mistake #2: Inconsistent Measurement

**Problem**: Data not comparable over time

**Solution**: Document calculation method, use same source

### Mistake #3: No Targets Set

**Problem**: Can't tell if performance is good or bad

**Solution**: Set realistic targets based on industry and goals

### Mistake #4: Not Acting on Data

**Problem**: Track KPIs but don't use them

**Solution**: Monthly review, identify issues, take action

### Mistake #5: Cherry-Picking Data

**Problem**: Only show good months, hide bad ones

**Solution**: Show all data, explain anomalies honestly

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: Don't have historical data

**Solutions**:
1. **Reconstruct from records**: Pull from accounting, CRM
2. **Start now**: Begin tracking today, build history forward
3. **Estimate conservatively**: Better than nothing, note as estimate

### Problem: KPI calculation is complex

**Solutions**:
1. **Use platform calculator**: Built-in formulas
2. **Simplify**: Use approximation if exact is too hard
3. **Get help**: Accountant or analyst can help

### Problem: Data sources don't match

**Solutions**:
1. **Pick one source**: Use most reliable
2. **Document discrepancies**: Explain differences
3. **Reconcile**: Figure out why they differ

### Problem: KPIs show declining performance

**Solutions**:
1. **Don't hide it**: Buyers will discover anyway
2. **Explain why**: Market conditions, one-time event, etc.
3. **Show recovery plan**: What you're doing to fix it
4. **Demonstrate improvement**: Recent months trending up
