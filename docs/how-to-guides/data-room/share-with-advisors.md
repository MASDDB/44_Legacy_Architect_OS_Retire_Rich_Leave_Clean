# How to Share Data Room Access

**Securely invite advisors and potential buyers to view your confidential documents.**

*Time required: 5 minutes*

---

## The Golden Rule of Sharing

**Never share just one password.**
Every person who enters your Data Room must have their own unique login. This allows you to:
- Track exactly who viewed what
- Revoke access for a specific person if a deal initially fails
- Maintain a secure audit trail

---

## Step 1: Invite a User

1. Navigate to **Data Room**.
2. Click the **"Users & Permissions"** button in the top right.
3. Click **"Invite User"**.
4. Enter the user's **Email Address**.
5. Assign a **Role**:
   - **Viewer**: Can view files but usually cannot download (controlled by permissions).
   - **Contributor**: Can upload files (good for your accountant/CPA).
   - **Admin**: Full control (sparingly used).

---

## Step 2: Set Granular Permissions

You control exactly which folders they can see.

**The Permission Matrix**:
When inviting a user (or editing them later), you will see a list of your top-level folders.

| Permission Level | Description | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **No Access** | Folder is invisible to them. | Competitors, early-stage buyers |
| **View Only** | Can read on-screen. Watermarked. No download. | Most buyers, sensitive files |
| **Download** | Can download the original PDF/Excel. | Final stage diligence, Advisors |
| **Edit/Upload** | Can add or delete files. | Your internal team only |

**Defaults**:
- We recommend starting new buyers with **"View Only"** access to the "Teaser" materials and **"No Access"** to sensitive financial details until an LOI is signed.

---

## Step 3: Add Watermarking (Recommended)

Protect your documents from leaking.

1. Ensure **"Enable Dynamic Watermarking"** is checked (this is usually a global setting or per-user).
2. **What it does**: Overlays the user's email address and the current date/time on every page they view or print.
3. **Why**: If a document leaks, you know exactly who leaked it.

---

## Step 4: Revoking Access

If a buyer walks away or a deal falls through:

1. Go to **Users & Permissions**.
2. Find the user in the list.
3. Click the **"..."** (More) menu.
4. Select **"Revoke Access"**.
   - *Instant Kill*: They are logged out immediately and cannot log back in.
   - *History Kept*: Their activity logs are preserved for your records.

---

## Best Practices

### The "Groups" Feature
Instead of setting permissions for every single person, create Groups:
1. Create a "Strategy Buyers" group.
2. Set permissions for the group once.
3. Add 5 different buyers to that group. They inherit the permissions.

### Monitor Activity
Check the **"Activity Log"** tab regularly.
- **High Interest**: A buyer who views the "Financials" folder 10 times in one day.
- **Low Interest**: A buyer who hasn't logged in for 2 weeks.
