# How to Organize Data Room Folders

**Structure your diligence materials to tell a compelling story and speed up the deal.**

*Time required: 15-20 minutes*

---

## Why Structure Matters

A chaotic Data Room screams "chaotic business" to buyers. A well-organized structure:
- **Builds Confidence**: Shows you are professional and prepared.
- **Reduces Questions**: Buyers can find what they need without emailing you.
- **Protects Value**: Prevents critical documents from being overlooked.

---

## Step 1: Use the Standard Folder Template

Legacy Architect OS provides a best-practice folder structure approved by investment bankers.

1. Navigate to **Data Room**.
2. Click **"New Folder"** if starting from scratch, or check your pre-populated folders.
3. The recommended top-level hierarchy is:

   1. **Corporate Documents** (Legal entity, bylaws, ownership)
   2. **Financials** (P&Ls, tax returns, audits)
   3. **Human Resources** (Org chart, key employee contracts)
   4. **Sales & Marketing** (Customer contracts, brochures, pricing)
   5. **Operations** (Supplier agreements, inventory lists)
   6. **Technology & IP** (Patents, software licenses)
   7. **Real Estate** (Leases, deeds)
   8. **Legal & Compliance** (Litigation history, licenses)

---

## Step 2: Create Sub-Folders

Keep specific documents grouped together.

**Example Structure for "2. Financials"**:
- `2.1 Annual Financials`
- `2.2 Monthly Reports`
- `2.3 Tax Returns`
- `2.4 Bank Statements`
- `2.5 Accounts Receivable Aging`

**How to create**:
1. Open the parent folder (e.g., "Financials").
2. Click **"New Folder"**.
3. Name it using the number prefix (e.g., "2.1 Annual Financials") to keep them sorted.

---

## Step 3: Implement Naming Conventions

Consistent filenames make the room easy to navigate.

**Best Practices**:
- **Use Dates**: `YYYY-MM-DD` or `YYYY` at the start.
- **Be Descriptive**: Don't upload "Scan123.pdf".
- **Use Versioning**: If a document changes, append `_v2`.

**Bad Example**: `P&L final.pdf`
**Good Example**: `2024_Annual_PnL_Statement.pdf`

---

## Step 4: Staging vs. Live Folders

You don't have to show everything at once.

1. **Staging Folder**: Create a folder named `_Staging` (the underscore keeps it at the top).
   - Use this for gathering documents before they are ready for buyers.
   - **Permission**: Set to "Admins Only".

2. **Moving Files**:
   - Once a document is reviewed and redacted (if needed), drag it from `_Staging` to the appropriate live folder (e.g., `2. Financials`).

---

## Step 5: Advanced Permissions (Optional)

You can hide sensitive folders from certain buyers.

1. Right-click a folder (e.g., "3. Human Resources").
2. Select **"Manage Permissions"**.
3. **Groups**:
   - **All Buyers**: Can View
   - **Competitors**: No Access (Revoke access for sensitive IP)
   - **Internal Team**: Edit

*Detailed permission settings are covered in the "Share with Advisors" guide.*

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Empty Folders**: Don't create a folder until you have a file to put in it. Empty folders frustrate users.
- **Too Deep**: Try not to go more than 3 levels deep (Folder > Sub-folder > File).
- **Loose Files**: Avoid putting files in the root directory. Everything should have a home.
