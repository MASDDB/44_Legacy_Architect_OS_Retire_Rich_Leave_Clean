# How to Complete the Exit Readiness Assessment

**Understand where your business stands and get personalized recommendations.**

*Time required: 20-30 minutes*

---

## What is the Exit Readiness Assessment?

The assessment evaluates your business across three critical dimensions:
- **Financial Readiness** (33% of score)
- **Operational Readiness** (33% of score)
- **Market Readiness** (33% of score)

**Output**: A scored report (0-100) with recommendations and valuation estimates.

---

## Before You Start

### What You'll Need

Have this information ready:
- **Financial data**: Revenue, margins, growth rates
- **Customer data**: Number of customers, concentration, retention
- **Operational data**: Team size, documented processes
- **Market data**: Competitive position, industry trends

### Set Aside Time

- **Don't rush**: 20-30 minutes uninterrupted
- **Be honest**: Accurate answers = better recommendations
- **Save progress**: Can pause and resume later

---

## Step 1: Access the Assessment

1. Log in to Legacy Architect OS
2. From dashboard, click **"Take Assessment"**
   - Or navigate to **"Exit Readiness"** in left sidebar
   - Click **"Start New Assessment"**

---

## Step 2: Business Basics (5 minutes)

### Questions in This Section

**Business Information**:
- Business name
- Industry and sub-industry
- Years in operation
- Legal structure (LLC, S-Corp, etc.)

**Size Metrics**:
- Annual revenue (most recent year)
- Number of employees (FTE)
- Number of locations

**Ownership**:
- Number of owners
- Your ownership percentage

### Tips for This Section

- **Be accurate**: This data affects benchmarking
- **Use full-time equivalents**: Count part-time as 0.5 FTE
- **Include yourself**: In employee count and ownership

---

## Step 3: Financial Metrics (10 minutes)

### Revenue Questions

**Annual Revenue**:
- Last year's revenue
- Year before that
- Growth rate calculation (auto-calculated)

**Revenue Quality**:
- Percentage of recurring revenue
- Percentage from top 10 customers
- Average customer lifetime value

### Profitability Questions

**Margins**:
- Gross margin percentage
- EBITDA margin percentage
- Net profit margin

**Cash Flow**:
- Positive or negative cash flow?
- Months of cash reserves
- Accounts receivable days

### Tips for Financial Section

**If you don't know exact numbers**:
- Use your best estimate
- Round to nearest $10K or $100K
- Mark "Approximate" if available

**Common mistakes**:
- Confusing revenue with profit
- Not including all revenue sources
- Forgetting to account for seasonality

**Where to find this data**:
- P&L statements
- Tax returns
- Accounting software (QuickBooks, Xero, etc.)
- Ask your accountant

---

## Step 4: Operational Readiness (10 minutes)

### Systems & Processes

**Documentation**:
- Do you have documented processes?
- How many processes are documented?
- Quality of documentation (basic notes vs. detailed SOPs)

**Technology**:
- What systems do you use? (CRM, accounting, project management)
- Are they integrated?
- Cloud-based or on-premise?

### Team & Management

**Management Team**:
- Do you have a management team?
- Can they run the business without you?
- How long have key people been with company?

**Owner Dependency**:
- How many hours/week do you work?
- What happens if you're gone for 2 weeks?
- What decisions require your approval?

### Customer Relationships

**Contracts**:
- Percentage of revenue under contract
- Average contract length
- Renewal rates

**Retention**:
- Customer retention rate
- Churn rate
- Net promoter score (if known)

### Tips for Operational Section

**Be honest about owner dependency**:
- Most businesses are owner-dependent
- Acknowledging it is the first step to fixing it
- Buyers will discover it anyway

**Document what you have**:
- Even basic documentation is better than none
- Note what's in your head vs. written down

---

## Step 5: Market Position (5 minutes)

### Competitive Landscape

**Market Position**:
- Are you a market leader, challenger, or niche player?
- What's your market share?
- Who are your top 3 competitors?

**Differentiation**:
- What makes you different/better?
- Do you have proprietary methods or IP?
- Barriers to entry for competitors?

### Growth Potential

**Market Trends**:
- Is your market growing, stable, or declining?
- What's the growth rate?
- Any disruptive threats?

**Strategic Value**:
- Would you be strategic acquisition for larger company?
- Do you have unique assets (customer list, IP, team)?
- Geographic expansion potential?

### Tips for Market Section

**Think like a buyer**:
- What would make YOUR business attractive?
- What risks would concern a buyer?
- What growth opportunities exist?

**Be realistic**:
- Don't overstate your market position
- Acknowledge competitive threats
- Honest assessment = better recommendations

---

## Step 6: Review and Submit

### Before Submitting

1. **Review your answers**
   - Click **"Review"** button
   - Check for obvious errors
   - Verify numbers make sense

2. **Add notes** (optional)
   - Explain unusual circumstances
   - Provide context for answers
   - Flag areas of uncertainty

3. **Save progress**
   - Click **"Save Draft"** if not ready to submit
   - Resume later from where you left off

### Submit Assessment

1. Click **"Submit Assessment"**
2. Confirm submission
3. Processing takes 10-30 seconds
4. Results appear automatically

## Understanding Your Results

### Overall Exit Readiness Score

**Score Range**: 0-100

**Interpretation**:
- **80-100**: Excellent - Ready for market
- **60-79**: Good - Minor improvements needed
- **40-59**: Fair - Significant work required
- **0-39**: Poor - Major gaps to address

### Category Scores

**Financial Score**:
- Revenue quality and growth
- Profitability and margins
- Customer concentration

**Operational Score**:
- Systems and processes
- Team and management
- Owner dependency

**Market Score**:
- Competitive position
- Growth potential
- Strategic value

### Valuation Estimates

**Estimated Range**:
- Low end: Conservative estimate
- High end: Optimistic estimate
- Based on your financials and industry multiples

**Factors Affecting Valuation**:
- Your Exit Readiness Score
- Industry multiples
- Revenue and EBITDA
- Growth rate
- Market conditions

### Recommendations

**Prioritized Action Items**:
- Top 5-10 recommendations
- Ranked by impact
- Quick wins vs. long-term improvements

**Suggested Missions**:
- Data Room setup
- Cash-Boost campaigns
- Process documentation
- Team development

### Download Your Report

3. Share with advisors (optional)

**What's in the PDF**:
- Your scores (overall and by category)
- Detailed breakdown
- Recommendations
- Valuation estimates
- Comparison to industry benchmarks

### Create Your Action Plan

1. Review recommendations
2. Pick top 3-5 to focus on
3. Create 90-day plan
4. Set milestones and deadlines

See: [Understanding Your Exit Readiness Score](../../tutorials/exit-readiness-score.md)

### Set Retake Reminder

**When to retake**:
- After 60-90 days of improvements
- Before engaging M&A advisors
- Quarterly (if actively preparing for sale)
- Annually (if long-term planning)

## Tips for Better Results

### Be Honest

**Don't inflate answers to get higher score**:
- You're only fooling yourself
- Buyers will discover the truth
- Better to know real gaps now

**Acknowledge weaknesses**:
- Every business has them
- Identifying them is first step to fixing
- Honest assessment = actionable recommendations

### Use Accurate Data

**Don't guess if you can look it up**:
- Check your accounting system
- Review tax returns
- Ask your accountant
- Use actual numbers, not estimates

**Round appropriately**:
- Revenue: Nearest $10K or $100K
- Percentages: Nearest 5%
- Counts: Exact numbers

### Provide Context

**Use the notes field**:
- Explain unusual circumstances
- Provide additional context
- Flag areas of uncertainty

**Example notes**:
- "Revenue dropped 20% due to COVID, but recovered in 2024"
- "Customer concentration high but working to diversify"
- "Owner hours high but hiring COO next quarter"

## Common Questions

### Can I pause and resume?

**Yes!** Click **"Save Draft"** anytime:
- Progress is saved
- Resume from dashboard
- No time limit to complete

### Can I change my answers?

**Before submitting**: Yes, edit anytime

**After submitting**: No, but you can:
- Take a new assessment
- Compare scores over time
- Track improvement

### How often should I take it?

**Recommended frequency**:
- **First time**: Baseline assessment
- **60-90 days later**: After initial improvements
- **Quarterly**: If actively preparing for sale
- **Annually**: If long-term planning

### Is my data private?

**Yes!** Your assessment data is:
- Visible only to you
- Not shared with anyone unless you choose
- Encrypted and secure
- Can be deleted anytime

### What if I don't know an answer?

**Options**:
1. **Skip it** - Some questions are optional
2. **Estimate** - Use your best guess
3. **Look it up** - Check your records
4. **Ask advisor** - Accountant, consultant, etc.

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: Can't submit assessment

**Solutions**:
1. **Check required fields** - All marked with * must be completed
2. **Verify numbers** - Must be valid (no negative revenue, etc.)
3. **Check internet connection** - Stable connection required
4. **Try different browser** - Chrome recommended

### Problem: Results seem wrong

**Check**:
1. **Review your answers** - Did you enter data correctly?
2. **Check units** - Revenue in dollars, not thousands?
3. **Verify calculations** - Growth rates, margins, etc.
4. **Compare to benchmarks** - Are you above/below industry average?

### Problem: Lost my progress

**Solutions**:
1. **Check drafts** - Look for saved draft on dashboard
2. **Log out and back in** - Sometimes refreshes data
3. **Contact support** - We may be able to recover
