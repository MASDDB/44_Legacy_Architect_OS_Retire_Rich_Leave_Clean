## Part 1: Your First Day – Get Clarity, Not Complexity

### 1. Log In and Run Your Exit Readiness Assessment

Your very first task is simple:

**Run the Exit Readiness Assessment.**

This is your "X-ray." You'll answer questions about:
- Revenue and profitability
- Lead flow and follow-up
- How calls are handled right now
- How dependent the business is on you
- What systems and documentation you already have (or don't)

**This is not about looking good on paper.** It's about getting an honest snapshot so the platform can point you in the right direction. You can't improve what you won't measure.

#### How to Complete the Assessment

1. From your dashboard, click **"Take Assessment"**
2. Set aside **20-30 minutes** of uninterrupted time
3. Answer honestly (the data is private to you)
4. Don't overthink it - your first instinct is usually correct
5. Click **"Submit"** when complete

#### What You'll Get

After submission, you'll receive:

- **Overall Exit Readiness Score** (0-100)
- **Category Breakdowns**: Financial, Operational, Market
- **Valuation Estimates**: Based on your inputs
- **Prioritized Recommendations**: What to fix first
- **Mission Suggestions**: Revenue-boosting opportunities

> **💡 Pro Tip**: Download your assessment report as a PDF. You'll want to reference it later and track your progress over time.

### 2. Review Your Score (Without Panicking)

Here's what your score means:

| Score Range | What It Means | What to Do |
|-------------|---------------|------------|
| **80-100** | Excellent - Ready for market | Focus on timing and finding the right buyer |
| **60-79** | Good - Minor improvements needed | Address top 3-5 gaps, then go to market |
| **40-59** | Fair - Significant work required | 6-12 month improvement plan |
| **0-39** | Poor - Major gaps to address | 12-24 month systematic rebuild |

**Important**: A low score is not a failure. It's a starting point. Most businesses score 40-60 on their first assessment.

The businesses that sell for top dollar aren't the ones that started perfect—they're the ones that **measured, improved, and measured again**.

### 3. Pick ONE Thing to Focus On

Your assessment will give you a list of recommendations. **Don't try to do them all at once.**

Instead, choose ONE of these paths based on your timeline:

#### Path A: Immediate Exit (1-2 Years)
**Focus**: Get buyer-ready ASAP

**Your ONE thing**: Start building your Data Room
- Set up the Folder 0-10 structure
- Upload your most critical documents
- Aim for 50% completion in 30 days

#### Path B: Near-Term Exit (3-5 Years)
**Focus**: Optimize value while preparing

**Your ONE thing**: Launch your first Cash-Boost Mission
- Generate immediate revenue
- Prove growth trajectory to future buyers
- Build momentum in the business

#### Path C: Long-Term Exit (5+ Years)
**Focus**: Build maximum value

**Your ONE thing**: Set up systematic tracking
- Enter your financial data
- Establish KPI tracking
- Create quarterly review habit

### 4. Set Your First Milestone

Before you close your laptop on Day 1, set a specific milestone:

**Examples**:
- "Upload 10 documents to Data Room by Friday"
- "Launch Cash-Boost campaign by end of week"
- "Enter last 12 months of financial data by next Monday"

Write it down. Put it in your calendar. Make it real.

## Part 2: Your First Week – Build the Foundation

### Day 2-3: Set Up Your Business Profile

Your business profile is the foundation for everything else.

#### What to Complete

1. **Basic Information**
   - Business name and legal entity
   - Industry and sub-industry
   - Website and contact info
   - Physical address

2. **Financial Overview**
   - Annual revenue (most recent year)
   - Number of employees
   - Year founded
   - Ownership structure

3. **Exit Goals**
   - Target exit timeline
   - Desired sale price range
   - Post-exit plans
   - Deal-breakers (what you won't compromise on)

> **Why this matters**: This information personalizes your experience and ensures recommendations are relevant to YOUR business, not a generic template.

### Day 4-5: Start Your Chosen Path

#### If You Chose: Data Room Setup

**Day 4 Tasks**:
1. Navigate to **Data Room** section
2. Review the Folder 0-10 structure
3. Identify which folders you can start filling immediately
4. Gather existing documents (don't create new ones yet)

**Day 5 Tasks**:
1. Upload 5-10 documents to appropriate folders
2. Connect Google Drive or Dropbox (if you have existing files there)
3. Note which folders are empty (you'll fill them later)

**Success Metric**: 20% Data Room completion by end of week

#### If You Chose: Cash-Boost Mission

**Day 4 Tasks**:
1. Navigate to **Missions** section
2. Click **"Cash-Boost Campaign"**
3. Review the campaign options:
   - Reactivation (dormant customers)
   - Upsell (existing customers)
   - Referral (happy customers)
4. Choose ONE campaign type

**Day 5 Tasks**:
1. Set up your campaign:
   - Define your offer
   - Set your target list
   - Schedule your outreach
2. Launch the campaign
3. Set up tracking

**Success Metric**: Campaign launched and first responses received

#### If You Chose: Financial Tracking

**Day 4 Tasks**:
1. Navigate to **Financials** section
2. Gather your P&L statements (last 12 months minimum)
3. Enter revenue and expense data for most recent month

**Day 5 Tasks**:
1. Enter remaining 11 months of data
2. Identify your top 5 KPIs to track
3. Set up monthly tracking reminders

**Success Metric**: 12 months of financial data entered

### Day 6-7: Invite Your Team (Optional)

If you have team members who should be involved:

1. Navigate to **Settings** → **Team**
2. Click **"Invite Team Member"**
3. Enter their email and assign role:
   - **Admin**: Full access (use sparingly)
   - **Manager**: Can view and edit most things
   - **Contributor**: Can upload documents and enter data
   - **Viewer**: Read-only access

**Who to invite**:
- Accountant/Bookkeeper (for financial data)
- Operations Manager (for process documentation)
- Sales Manager (for CRM and lead data)
- Legal Counsel (for contract review)

> **💡 Pro Tip**: Don't invite everyone at once. Start with 1-2 key people and expand as you get comfortable with the platform.

## Part 3: Your First Month – Create Your New Normal

### Week 2: Deep Dive on Your Chosen Path

#### Data Room Path

**Goals for Week 2**:
- [ ] Reach 40% Data Room completion
- [ ] Complete at least 3 folders entirely
- [ ] Identify missing documents you need to create

**Focus Folders** (prioritize these):
- **Folder 1**: Corporate Documents (articles, bylaws, ownership)
- **Folder 2**: Financial Information (P&Ls, balance sheets)
- **Folder 6**: Customer & Contracts (top 10 customers, key contracts)

**Time Investment**: 2-3 hours this week

#### Cash-Boost Path

**Goals for Week 2**:
- [ ] Track campaign responses
- [ ] Follow up with interested prospects
- [ ] Calculate early ROI
- [ ] Adjust campaign based on results

**Key Metrics to Watch**:
- Open rate (emails)
- Response rate
- Conversion rate
- Revenue generated

**Time Investment**: 3-4 hours this week

#### Financial Tracking Path

**Goals for Week 2**:
- [ ] Enter 2-3 years of historical financial data
- [ ] Set up KPI tracking dashboard
- [ ] Identify your EBITDA add-backs
- [ ] Document top 3 add-backs with evidence

**Key Activities**:
- Review past tax returns
- Identify owner expenses in the business
- Document one-time expenses
- Calculate normalized EBITDA

**Time Investment**: 4-5 hours this week

### Week 3: Expand Your Scope

Now that you've built momentum in one area, it's time to add a second focus.

#### If You Started with Data Room
**Add**: Financial tracking
- Enter your financial data
- Set up KPI dashboard
- This will help you see what documents you're missing

#### If You Started with Cash-Boost
**Add**: Data Room setup
- Start documenting your campaign results
- Upload campaign materials to Data Room
- Buyers will want to see your growth initiatives

#### If You Started with Financials
**Add**: Data Room setup
- Upload your financial documents
- Start organizing other critical files
- You already have the data, now organize it

**Time Investment**: 2-3 hours on your new focus area

### Week 4: Establish Your Rhythm

By week 4, you should establish a sustainable rhythm:

#### Weekly Habits to Build

**Monday Morning** (15 minutes):
- Review dashboard
- Check Mission progress
- Review any team activity

**Wednesday Afternoon** (30 minutes):
- Upload new documents as they're created
- Update financial data
- Respond to any RFIs or questions

**Friday End-of-Day** (20 minutes):
- Review week's progress
- Set next week's goals
- Update any tracking metrics

#### Monthly Habits to Build

**First Monday of Month** (1 hour):
- Enter previous month's financial data
- Update KPI tracking
- Review Exit Readiness Score progress

**Last Friday of Month** (30 minutes):
- Review Data Room completion %
- Check Mission ROI
- Plan next month's priorities

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Pitfall #1: Trying to Do Everything at Once

**The Problem**: You see all the features and try to use them all immediately.

**The Solution**: Pick ONE path and go deep for 30 days. You can add more later.

### Pitfall #2: Perfectionism Paralysis

**The Problem**: You won't upload a document until it's "perfect" or "final."

**The Solution**: Upload what you have. You can always replace it later. Progress > Perfection.

### Pitfall #3: Treating It Like a One-Time Project

**The Problem**: You do a bunch of work in week 1, then never log in again.

**The Solution**: Build weekly habits. Even 30 minutes/week compounds over time.

### Pitfall #4: Not Inviting Your Team

**The Problem**: You try to do everything yourself and burn out.

**The Solution**: Delegate data entry, document uploads, and tracking to appropriate team members.

### Pitfall #5: Ignoring the Assessment Results

**The Problem**: You complete the assessment but don't act on the recommendations.

**The Solution**: Pick your top 3 recommendations and create a 90-day plan to address them.
