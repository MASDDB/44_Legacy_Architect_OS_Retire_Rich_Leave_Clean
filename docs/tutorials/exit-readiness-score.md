## What the Score Measures

### The Three Pillars of Exit Readiness

Your overall score (0-100) is calculated from three equally weighted categories:

#### 1. Financial Readiness (33% of total score)

**What it measures**:
- Revenue quality and growth trajectory
- Profitability and margins
- Customer concentration and diversification
- Recurring revenue percentage
- Cash flow predictability
- Financial documentation quality

**Why it matters**: Buyers pay for predictable, growing cash flow. Strong financials = higher multiples.

#### 2. Operational Readiness (33% of total score)

**What it measures**:
- Systems and documented processes
- Management team depth and capability
- Owner dependency (can it run without you?)
- Scalability and capacity
- Technology and infrastructure
- Quality of record-keeping

**Why it matters**: Buyers want businesses that can operate independently. Less owner dependency = higher value.

#### 3. Market Readiness (33% of total score)

**What it measures**:
- Competitive positioning and differentiation
- Market growth trends and opportunity
- Customer retention and satisfaction
- Brand strength and reputation
- Barriers to entry for competitors
- Strategic value to acquirers

**Why it matters**: Buyers pay premiums for businesses with competitive advantages and growth potential.

## How Scoring Works

### The Calculation Formula

```
Overall Score = (Financial Score + Operational Score + Market Score) / 3
```

Each category score is calculated from your responses to specific questions:

#### Financial Score Breakdown

| Component | Weight | What It Evaluates |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Revenue Growth | 25% | Year-over-year growth rate |
| Profitability | 25% | EBITDA margin and trends |
| Customer Concentration | 20% | Revenue from top 10 customers |
| Recurring Revenue | 15% | Percentage of predictable revenue |
| Cash Flow | 15% | Consistency and strength |

#### Operational Score Breakdown

| Component | Weight | What It Evaluates |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Documented Processes | 25% | SOPs and systems documentation |
| Management Team | 25% | Depth and capability of leadership |
| Owner Dependency | 20% | Business ability to run without owner |
| Technology Systems | 15% | Quality of tech infrastructure |
| Scalability | 15% | Capacity for growth |

#### Market Score Breakdown

| Component | Weight | What It Evaluates |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Competitive Position | 30% | Differentiation and advantages |
| Market Growth | 25% | Industry trends and opportunity |
| Customer Retention | 20% | Loyalty and repeat business |
| Brand Strength | 15% | Recognition and reputation |
| Strategic Value | 10% | Attractiveness to acquirers |

## Interpreting Your Score

### Score Ranges and What They Mean

#### 90-100: Exceptional (Top 5%)

**What it means**: Your business is in the top tier of sellable companies. You're ready for market now.

**Typical characteristics**:
- Strong, consistent revenue growth (15%+ annually)
- EBITDA margins above industry average
- Diversified customer base (no customer >10% of revenue)
- Professional management team in place
- Documented systems and processes
- Strong competitive moat
- High customer retention (90%+)

**Valuation impact**: Expect premium multiples (top 25% for your industry)

**Next steps**:
- Engage M&A advisors
- Prepare for buyer conversations
- Focus on timing and finding the right buyer
- Maintain momentum while in process

#### 80-89: Excellent (Top 15%)

**What it means**: You're very close to market-ready with only minor gaps to address.

**Typical characteristics**:
- Good revenue growth (10-15% annually)
- Solid profitability
- Some customer concentration to address
- Management team mostly in place
- Most processes documented
- Clear competitive advantages

**Valuation impact**: Above-average multiples

**Next steps**:
- Address top 3-5 gaps
- Consider soft market testing
- 3-6 month preparation timeline
- Build relationships with potential buyers

#### 70-79: Good (Top 30%)

**What it means**: You're on the right track but need focused improvements before going to market.

**Typical characteristics**:
- Moderate revenue growth (5-10% annually)
- Acceptable profitability
- Some customer concentration issues
- Key person risk (owner dependency)
- Some undocumented processes
- Competitive but not dominant

**Valuation impact**: Market-rate multiples

**Next steps**:
- 6-12 month improvement plan
- Focus on reducing owner dependency
- Document critical processes
- Diversify customer base
- Strengthen management team

#### 60-69: Fair (Top 50%)

**What it means**: You have a sellable business, but significant work is needed to maximize value.

**Typical characteristics**:
- Flat or slow revenue growth (<5% annually)
- Margins under pressure
- High customer concentration
- Heavy owner dependency
- Limited documented systems
- Competitive but not differentiated

**Valuation impact**: Below-average multiples (or difficulty finding buyers)

**Next steps**:
- 12-18 month systematic improvement plan
- Address critical gaps first
- Consider bringing in advisors
- Focus on one category at a time
- Retake assessment quarterly

#### 40-59: Needs Work (Bottom 50%)

**What it means**: Your business has value, but isn't ready for a quality exit. Major improvements needed.

**Typical characteristics**:
- Declining or stagnant revenue
- Low or negative profitability
- Very high customer concentration
- Business can't operate without owner
- Few documented processes
- Weak competitive position

**Valuation impact**: Significantly reduced multiples (or unsellable)

**Next steps**:
- 18-24 month rebuild plan
- Consider bringing in turnaround expertise
- Focus on profitability first
- Address existential risks
- May need to pivot strategy

#### 0-39: Critical Issues (Bottom 20%)

**What it means**: Fundamental problems exist that make the business difficult or impossible to sell.

**Typical characteristics**:
- Declining revenue
- Unprofitable or barely profitable
- One or few customers (extreme concentration)
- Owner IS the business
- No systems or documentation
- Commoditized offering

**Valuation impact**: May be unsellable in current state

**Next steps**:
- Honest assessment: Fix or exit?
- If fixing: 24+ month complete rebuild
- Address survival issues first
- Consider strategic alternatives
- May need professional help

## How to Improve Your Score

### The 90-Day Improvement Cycle

#### Step 1: Identify Your Weakest Category

Look at your three category scores. Which is lowest?

- **Financial**: Focus on revenue growth and profitability
- **Operational**: Focus on systems and reducing owner dependency
- **Market**: Focus on competitive positioning and customer retention

#### Step 2: Pick Your Top 3 Gaps

Within that category, identify the 3 specific areas with the lowest scores.

**Example** (Operational category):
1. No documented processes (scored 2/10)
2. Heavy owner dependency (scored 3/10)
3. Weak management team (scored 4/10)

#### Step 3: Create 90-Day Action Plans

For each gap, create a specific, measurable plan:

**Gap**: No documented processes

**90-Day Plan**:
- Week 1-2: Document top 5 critical processes
- Week 3-4: Create process templates
- Week 5-8: Train team on documented processes
- Week 9-12: Refine based on feedback, document next 5 processes

**Success Metric**: 10 core processes documented and in use

#### Step 4: Execute and Track

- Set weekly milestones
- Track progress in Legacy Architect OS
- Adjust plan as needed
- Document improvements for buyers

#### Step 5: Retake Assessment

After 90 days:
- Retake the Exit Readiness Assessment
- Compare scores (overall and by category)
- Celebrate improvements
- Identify next set of gaps to address

### Category-Specific Improvement Strategies

#### Improving Financial Score

**Quick Wins** (30-90 days):
- Clean up your books (hire good bookkeeper if needed)
- Document EBITDA add-backs with evidence
- Set up monthly financial reporting
- Track key metrics (CAC, LTV, churn, etc.)

**Medium-Term** (3-6 months):
- Launch revenue growth initiatives (Cash-Boost Missions)
- Improve margins (pricing, cost reduction)
- Diversify customer base (reduce concentration)
- Increase recurring revenue percentage

**Long-Term** (6-12 months):
- Achieve consistent 10%+ annual growth
- Improve EBITDA margins by 5-10 points
- Get top customer below 10% of revenue
- Build 3-6 months cash reserves

#### Improving Operational Score

**Quick Wins** (30-90 days):
- Document your top 10 processes
- Create an org chart
- Identify key person dependencies
- Set up basic project management system

**Medium-Term** (3-6 months):
- Hire or promote key management roles
- Implement process documentation system
- Reduce owner involvement in day-to-day
- Upgrade critical technology systems

**Long-Term** (6-12 months):
- Build complete management team
- Document all critical processes
- Achieve 2-week "owner vacation test"
- Implement scalable systems

#### Improving Market Score

**Quick Wins** (30-90 days):
- Survey customers for satisfaction scores
- Document your competitive advantages
- Create case studies of success stories
- Improve online reputation (reviews)

**Medium-Term** (3-6 months):
- Launch customer retention initiatives
- Strengthen brand presence
- Develop strategic partnerships
- Create barriers to entry

**Long-Term** (6-12 months):
- Achieve 90%+ customer retention
- Build recognized brand in your niche
- Develop proprietary methods or IP
- Position as strategic acquisition target

## When to Retake the Assessment

### Recommended Frequency

#### If Your Score is 0-59
**Retake every 60 days**

Why: You're making major improvements and want to track progress closely.

#### If Your Score is 60-79
**Retake every 90 days**

Why: You're making steady improvements and quarterly tracking makes sense.

#### If Your Score is 80-100
**Retake every 6-12 months**

Why: You're maintaining excellence and checking for any slippage.

### Before Retaking

1. **Review your previous assessment** - What were your weak areas?
2. **Document improvements made** - What have you actually done?
3. **Gather updated data** - Current financials, metrics, etc.
4. **Be honest** - Don't inflate answers to look better

### After Retaking

1. **Compare scores** - Overall and by category
2. **Celebrate wins** - Even small improvements matter
3. **Identify new gaps** - What's next to work on?
4. **Update your action plan** - Next 90 days
5. **Share with advisors** - If applicable

## Using Your Score Strategically

### For Exit Planning

Your score helps you determine your realistic timeline:

- **80-100**: Ready now (0-6 months to market)
- **70-79**: Nearly ready (6-12 months preparation)
- **60-69**: Need work (12-18 months preparation)
- **40-59**: Major work needed (18-24 months)
- **0-39**: Fundamental rebuild (24+ months)

### For Valuation Negotiations

A higher score = stronger negotiating position:

- **Document your improvements** - Show the trajectory
- **Highlight strengths** - Use high-scoring areas as selling points
- **Address weaknesses proactively** - Don't let buyers discover them
- **Show commitment to excellence** - Regular assessments demonstrate professionalism

### For Team Motivation

Share appropriate scores with your team:

- **Celebrate improvements** - Team effort pays off
- **Set team goals** - "Let's get to 75 by Q3"
- **Assign ownership** - Different team members own different categories
- **Track progress together** - Monthly or quarterly reviews

## Common Misconceptions

### Misconception #1: "I need a perfect score to sell"

**Reality**: Most businesses sell with scores in the 70-85 range. Perfection isn't required.

### Misconception #2: "My score is too low to ever sell"

**Reality**: Scores can improve dramatically with focused effort. We've seen 30+ point improvements in 12 months.

### Misconception #3: "The score doesn't matter, only the price"

**Reality**: Your score directly impacts the price you'll get and the quality of buyers you'll attract.

### Misconception #4: "I can just fix things during due diligence"

**Reality**: Buyers discount heavily for issues discovered during due diligence. Fix them beforehand.

### Misconception #5: "My industry is different, the score doesn't apply"

**Reality**: The fundamentals (growth, profitability, systems, team) apply across all industries.
