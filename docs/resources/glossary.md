# Master Glossary

**Key terms and definitions used in Legacy Architect OS and the M&A process.**

---

## A
**Accrual Basis Accounting**: Recording revenue when earned and expenses when incurred, regardless of when cash changes hands. Preferred by buyers over Cash Basis.
**Add-Backs**: Expenses added back to net income to calculate Adjusted EBITDA (e.g., owner's personal car, one-time legal fees).
**Adjusted EBITDA**: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization, adjusted for non-recurring items. The primary metric for valuing a business.

## B
**Balance Sheet**: A financial statement showing assets, liabilities, and equity at a specific point in time.
**Blue Sky**: The future growth potential of a business that a buyer is paying for.
**Book-to-Tax Reconciliation**: A document explaining the difference between the profit shown on financial statements (or "books") and the profit reported to the IRS.

## C
**Cash Basis Accounting**: Recording revenue only when cash is received and expenses only when paid. Common for small businesses but discouraged for exits.
**CIM (Confidential Information Memorandum)**: A detailed marketing document provided to potential buyers after they sign an NDA.
**Churn Rate**: The percentage of customers who stop doing business with you over a given period. Low churn increases valuation.

## D
**Data Room**: A secure online repository where confidential documents are stored and shared with buyers during due diligence.
**Deal Mode**: The active phase of selling a business, characterized by intense due diligence and rapid response times.
**Due Diligence**: The audit and investigation process a buyer performs to confirm the facts of a deal.

## E
**Earnout**: A provision where part of the purchase price is paid later, contingent on the business hitting future performance targets.
**EBITDA**: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. A proxy for cash flow.
**Exit Readiness Score**: A proprietary Legacy Architect OS metric (0-100) indicating how prepared a business is for sale.

## G
**GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles)**: The standard accounting rules used in the US.
**Granular Permissions**: The ability to control access to specific folders or files for specific users.

## I
**Indication of Interest (IOI)**: A non-binding letter from a buyer expressing serious interest and a price range.
**IP (Intellectual Property)**: Intangible assets like patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets.

## K
**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**: Measurable values that demonstrate how effectively a company is achieving key business objectives.

## L
**Letter of Intent (LOI)**: A formal document outlining the terms of a deal. Usually signifies the start of exclusivity and deep due diligence.

## M
**Multiple**: The factor applied to a metric (usually EBITDA) to determine the business's value (e.g., "5x EBITDA").

## N
**NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)**: A legal contract protecting confidential information shared during negotiations.

## Q
**Quality of Earnings (QofE)**: A detailed financial analysis, typically performed by a third-party accounting firm, to verify the accuracy of a company's historical earnings.

## R
**Recurring Revenue**: Revenue that is predictable, stable, and likely to continue in the future (e.g., subscriptions). Highly valued by buyers.

## T
**Tail Coverage**: Insurance coverage for claims made after a policy (and the business sale) has ended.
**Teaser**: A one or two-page anonymous summary of the business used to attract initial buyer interest.
**Trailing Twelve Months (TTM)**: Financial data for the most recent 12-month period.

## W
**Watermarking**: Overlaying a user's identity on a document to deter unauthorized sharing.
**Working Capital**: Current Assets minus Current Liabilities. A measure of a company's short-term financial health.
**Working Capital Peg**: The target amount of working capital a seller is expected to leave in the business at closing.
